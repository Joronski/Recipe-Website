Recipe 1: Special Cheesy Giniling

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 kilo Ground Pork
  • 1/2 cup Potatoes
  • 1/2 cup Carrots
  • 1/4 cup Green Peas
  • 1/4 cup Bell Pepper
  • 1/4 cup Cheese
  • 1 pc Onion
  • 5 cloves Garlic
  • 200 grams Tomato Sauce
  • 1 cup Water
  • 1/2 tbsp Sugar
  • 1 tbsp Butter
  • 2-3 tbsp Cooking Oil
  • Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • Quail Eggs (Optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare all the needed ingredients.
  2. Saut√©ed the onions and garlic and add the pork giniling.
  3. Cook it until juice from pork dries up, next is to add potato, carrots and green peas.
  4. Add the bell pepper and mix it well.
  5. Add butter and salt and then dash some black pepper.
  6. Add tomato sauce and water.
  7. Then simmer it for 2-3 minutes.
  8. Add tomato sauce again and add some cheese on it.
  9. Cook until cheese melt then taste it.
  10. After tasting dash some black pepper and add quail eggs.

Cooking Time: 20 minutes

Servings: 4

Recipe 2: Chicken Adobo

Ingredients:

  • 1800 grams Chicken Drumsticks (about 16 drumsticks)
  • 4 Bay Leaves
  • 70 grams Garlic (1 head, peeled)
  • 2 tsp Black Peppercorns
  • 3/4 cup Coconut Vinegar
  • 1/2 cup Soy Sauce
  • 1 pc Onion
  • 5 cloves Garlic
  • 200 grams Tomato Sauce
  • 1 cup Water
  • 1 tbsp Coconut Sugar

Instructions:

  1. Add the bay leaves, garlic, black peppercorns, coconut vinegar, soy sauce and coconut sugar in a resealable freezer bag or other airtight container, and then shake to combine.
  2. Add the chicken and seal the bag, pressing out as much air as you can. If using a container, make sure the chicken is submerged in the marinade. Let the chicken marinate in the fridge overnight, mixing it around a few times if you can.
  3. When you're ready to make the chicken adobo, pre-heat the oven to 280 degrees F (140 C).
  4. Open up the bag or container and pour its contents into a heavy bottomed pot with a lid (like a dutch oven). Close the lid and put the pot in the oven for 45 minutes.
  5. Remove the chicken from the oven and rearrange so that the chicken on top goes to the bottom. Braise in the oven for another 45 minutes or until the chicken is very tender.
  6. When the chicken is done, remove it from the oven and turn the oven up to 450 degrees F (230 C).
  7. Transfer the chicken to an oiled non-stick sheet pan and put it in the oven to caramelize until auburn brown (about 5 minutes).
  8. Meanwhile, boil the sauce until it becomes thick enough to coat the back of a spoon.
  9. When the chicken is done, put it back in the pot and toss to coat with the adobo sauce. Serve your chicken adobo garnished with scallions on a bed of hot white rice.

Cooking Time: 1 hr 50 mins

Servings: 6

Recipe 3: Sinigang na Baboy (Filipino Pork Sinigang Soup with Vegetables)

Ingredients:

  • 3/4 cups Tamarind Paste (homemade or store bought)
  • 1 lb Pork Spare Ribs
  • 1 lb Pork Belly (cut into 1/2 inch cubes)
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion (chopped)
  • 10 cloves Garlic (minced)
  • 5 small Tomatoes (roma sized - quartered)
  • 8 cups Filtered Water
  • 200 grams Tomato Sauce
  • 1 cup Water
  • 2 2/3 tbsp Salt (and more as needed)
  • 1 tbsp Fish Sauce
  • 1 small Eggplant (cut into 3" pieces)
  • 7 pieces Okra (cut into 1/2" pieces)
  • 4 inches daikon radish (cut in 1/4" slices, then cut into half rounds)
  • 7 String Beans (cut into 3" pieces)
  • 30 Stems Kang Kong / Water Spinach / On Choy
  • 1/2 tsp pepper (to taste)
  • Lime Juice (optional for more sourness)
  • 1 Finger Chile Pepper (optional for spiciness)

Instructions:

  1. Make your own tamarind paste using my instructions, or prepare store-bought tamarind paste, rehydrating if necessary.
  2. Pat the pork dry with a paper towel to improve browning during searing.
  3. In a large pot over medium heat, add the vegetable oil and begin to sear your meat in batches until all the sides have a nice golden brown crust. This should be about 2-5 minutes per batch. Try to not crowd the meat while searing. Transfer the meat onto a clean plate.
  4. In the same pot, add the onions and saute for about one minute or until the onions are slightly translucent. Add the garlic and saute for 30 seconds or until it‚Äôs fragrant.
  5. Add 2 cups of filtered water to deglaze the pot thoroughly. Gently scrape the golden brown parts from the searing meat at the bottom of the pan.
  6. Add the meat back to the pot with only 4 tomatoes (reserve the 5th one for later). Add 2 tablespoons of salt and stir to dissolve.
  7. Add 6 cups of water or enough so the meat is completely submerged. Bring the heat up to medium-high heat, once the liquid hits a boil let it boil for about 30 seconds.
  8. Drop the heat to a simmer (about medium-low heat) and simmer for about 45 minutes to 1 hour or until the meat is tender to your liking.
  9. Add the tamarind paste, pepper, and fish sauce to the pot and stir.
  10. Add the last tomato, eggplant, okra, string beans, and daikon radish into the pot and stir. Simmer for 5-8 minutes or until vegetables are tender, then add kang kong and cook for an additional one minute.
  11. Taste the soup and adjust with salt, pepper, or lime juice based on your preference. Serve immediately with a side of rice.

Cooking Time: 2 hrs

Servings: 8

Recipe 4: Ginisang Munggo (Monggo)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup whole monggo (mung) beans
  • 5 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 7 cloves garlic, lightly mashed
  • 2 medium-sized onions, chopped
  • 3/4 lb red-ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 lb malunggay leaves (if you can‚Äôt get this, substitute with spinach or kangkong. We used to grow kamote (sweet potatoes) in the back yard and we would just get kamote leaves and put it in the monggo)
  • 1 tsp salt or to taste
  • 1 tbsp calamansi or lime juice
  • calamansi, or lime, cut into wedges
  • shrimp and chicharon

Instructions:

  1. Clean and pick over the beans.
  2. Wash in several changes of water. Drain.
  3. Put the beans and 5 cups water into a 2-quart pot and bring to boil.
  4. Cover, lower heat and simmer for 2 minutes.
  5. Turn off the heat, and let the pot sit, covered for 1 hr.
  6. Bring the beans to a boil again.
  7. Turn heat to low and simmer gently for 1 1/2 hours or until the beans are tender and slightly mushy.
  8. Stir gently during the last half hour of cooking to prevent sticking.
  9. Saute garlic in the veg oil until light brown.
  10. Add onions, fry until translucent.
  11. Add tomatoes, stir for 5-6 minutes.
  12. Add cooked monggo beans and bring to a simmer.
  13. Simmer, with occasional stirring for 5 minutes.
  14. Add malunggay leaves, salt and 1 tbsp calamansi juice.
  15. Stir and simmer.
  16. Simmer with occasional stirring, 5 minutes or until leaves are done.
  17. Serve with chicharon, shrimp, calamansi wedges and rice.

Cooking Time: 1 hrs 10 mins

Servings: 5

Recipe 5: Tender Oxtail and Beef Kare Kare Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp of vegetable oil
  • 450g of oxtail
  • 450g of beef shank
  • 1 yellow onion, chopped
  • 5 tbsp of garlic, minced
  • 475ml of water
  • 1 tbsp of salt
  • 60g of peanuts, unsalted and dry
  • 45g of uncooked white rice
  • 2 Japanese eggplants, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 banana heart, peeled and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 5 string beans, cut into 4-inch pieces
  • 2 heads of bok choy, peeled and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 tbsp of vegetable oil
  • 1 1/2 yellow onion, chopped
  • 5 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp of annatto powder
  • 70g of smooth peanut butter
  • 1 tbsp of fish sauce
  • 1 3/4 tsp of salt
  • 1/2 tsp of black pepper

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, heat the oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add in the oxtail and beef shank, searing them on each side for a minute until they have a nice brown crust. Set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add in the chopped onions and saut√© until slightly translucent.
  4. Add the garlic and saut√© until fragrant.
  5. Add water into the pot, the meat, and salt and bring it to a boil.
  6. Turn the heat down to medium-low, cover with the lid, and let it simmer and braise for 3 hours.
  7. In a blender, add peanuts and blend until it reaches a fine powder consistency.
  8. In a pan over medium heat, add the rice and toast until lightly brown on the edges.
  9. In a clean blender, add the rice and blend until powdery.
  10. In another pot, add water and bring it to a boil.
  11. Throw in the eggplants, banana hearts, string beans and bok choy and blanch until slightly tender. Transfer it to an ice bath after.
  12. In a separate pot, add the oil over medium heat.
  13. Add in chopped onions and garlic and saut√©.
  14. Add in only the braising liquid, annatto powder and peanut butter. Stir until combined.
  15. Add the ground peanut powder, toasted rice powder and fish sauce and mix until all the ingredients are combined.
  16. Transfer the braised meat into the pot and season with salt and pepper.
  17. Turn the heat down to low and simmer for 15 minutes to let the sauce thicken.
  18. Add in the blanched vegetables and cook for another 3 minutes while stirring.
  19. Ladle into a bowl and serve.

Cooking Time: 3 hrs 40 mins

Servings: 4
